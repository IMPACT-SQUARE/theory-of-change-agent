# Benchmark concept brief — synthetic sample (Mode A input)

> A plain-language project brief a user might bring to Mode A (concept-first). **Fully synthetic — not
> based on any real project.** It deliberately describes the problem/target/approach WITHOUT any PDM
> structure, level labels, or indicator tables, so the agent must do the results-chain decomposition
> itself. This is a structural sanity check, not a measure of interview skill.

## The idea

We're a development-cooperation team planning a four-year project to improve maternal and newborn health
in a rural district of a partner country. Today many mothers give birth at home without skilled care,
the local primary clinics are short of trained staff and basic delivery equipment, and families have
little reliable information about prenatal and postnatal care.

We want to (a) train community health workers and midwives, (b) upgrade a set of primary clinics with
basic delivery and newborn-care equipment, and (c) run community awareness sessions on prenatal/postnatal
care. We expect that, with trained staff + equipped clinics + better-informed families, more mothers
will use skilled, facility-based care and adopt healthier prenatal/postnatal practices — reducing risks
for mothers and newborns.

Total budget is about ₩2.5bn over four years, delivered with a partner-country health-ministry counterpart
that provides facilities and seconded personnel. We ultimately want to contribute to healthy lives and
well-being in the district (SDG 3).

## What the agent should be able to derive (structural sanity check)
- A results chain whose **outputs** are: trained health workers, upgraded primary clinics, and a community
  awareness program.
- **Behavioral-change outcomes** around increased facility-based delivery and adoption of prenatal/
  postnatal care practices in the target population.
- A **strategic impact** tied to SDG 3 (health & well-being) — with no impact-level indicators.
- **Assumptions** about health-worker retention, family participation, and the equipment supply chain.
